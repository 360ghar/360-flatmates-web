# Product

## Register

product

## Users

360 Flatmates serves Indian renters and room posters, primarily young professionals in cities such as Bangalore, Gurugram, Mumbai, Hyderabad, and Pune. Users arrive with time pressure: they need to find a compatible flatmate, fill a room, schedule visits, or manage listing conversations without losing context across search, chat, and profile flows. Admin users review listings, reports, blog content, and trust/safety queues.

## Product Purpose

The product helps people find compatible flatmates and verified shared-living listings, using lifestyle compatibility, structured profiles, verified listings, search filters, chat, and visit scheduling. Public pages support acquisition and SEO, while authenticated pages support repeat task workflows: onboarding, search, swipe, match, chat, visit, listing creation, listing management, settings, payments, and moderation.

## Brand Personality

Warm, trustworthy, practical. The interface should feel human and approachable without slowing down task completion. Copy should be clear, direct, and safety-aware, especially around identity, payments, reports, and visit scheduling.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic property-portal clutter, over-decorated SaaS dashboards, decorative motion, vague empty states, hidden recovery paths, and any UI that makes safety or payment actions feel casual. Do not make public acquisition pages fight the app’s task-first product surface.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the task visible: every screen should make the next action obvious without hiding important context.
2. Preserve trust under failure: loading, empty, error, auth, and retry states must be explicit and recoverable.
3. Match on lifestyle, not just inventory: compatibility, profile quality, and listing context should stay close to decisions.
4. Use familiar product patterns: standard forms, modals, tabs, buttons, and lists should behave predictably.
5. Treat safety as a first-class workflow: report, block, verification, auth, and moderation controls should be easy to find and hard to misunderstand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for product surfaces. Interactive controls should meet mobile touch target expectations, have visible focus states, and expose accessible names. Motion must respect reduced-motion preferences. Color must never be the only carrier of status, especially for match scores, moderation states, errors, and payment outcomes.
